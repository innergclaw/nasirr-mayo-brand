Host Interview Sheet

INNERGNAS Q&A 2026

A clean interviewer sheet for conversations about AI, the future of work, agent systems, and what everyday people actually need to understand right now. Built from Nasirr Mayo’s perspective as an AI educator, digital strategist, founder of InnerG Intel, and builder across education, web systems, and real business workflows.

Use Case
Podcast, livestream, panel, interview, community talk
Core Themes
AI literacy, agents, future of work, readiness, real-world adoption
Best Tone
Clear, practical, visionary, no hype-noise
Opening framing for the host

Use these questions to guide a conversation that helps regular people, creators, freelancers, and business owners understand what AI means now, what agents change next, and how to prepare without getting lost in fear or jargon.

Main questions
Question 01
A lot of people still think AI just means ChatGPT. How do you explain what AI actually is today — and what it’s evolving into?
Follow-up angle: Let Nasirr define the landscape in plain language before the conversation gets deeper.
Question 02
What’s the real difference between using AI like a tool and using AI agents like a system?
Follow-up angle: This opens the door to workflows, automation, memory, task execution, and where things stop being “just prompts.”
Question 03
You’ve been pushing the idea of agent readiness. What does that mean, and why are so many people excited about AI but still unprepared for it?
Follow-up angle: Great setup for InnerG Intel’s education + consulting perspective.
Question 04
Over the next two to five years, how do you think AI will change life for creators, freelancers, and small business owners in real everyday terms?
Follow-up angle: Pull examples from content, operations, customer support, web work, sales, and personal productivity.
Question 05
What are the biggest myths or fears people have about AI right now — and what should they really be paying attention to instead?
Follow-up angle: This helps shift the conversation from fear narratives to strategy and discernment.
Question 06
If someone wants to prepare for the future of AI without getting overwhelmed, what are the first three moves they should make right now?
Follow-up angle: Strong closing question. Actionable. Useful for the masses.
Optional closer / viral clip question

Bonus question: Do you think AI will replace people — or is the real shift that people who know how to work with AI will replace people who don’t?

Host notes
  • Keep the conversation practical. The audience should leave smarter, not just impressed.
  • Ask for examples from real businesses, creators, and systems — not just theory.
  • Push into what preparation looks like, because that’s where the real value is.
  • If time is short, ask Questions 1, 3, and 6 for the strongest arc.
